ProteoCast for Drosophila melanogaster

Here we provide ProteoCast predictions and visualization for Drosophila melanogaster proteome, including all protein isoforms (proteoforms) resulting from alternative splicing.

Try it out with your favorite protein. Provide FlyBase protein ID (FBpp) or proteoform symbol (i.e. yki-PA) in the top-right search dialog.

For more information and resources, please refer to the documentation.

How it works:

  • Proteome-wide predictions are made by using the CollabFold (Mirdita et al., 2022) implementation of MMseqs2 (Steinegger and SÃ¶ding, 2017) to generate Multiple Sequences Alignements (MSA). These MSA are then used as an input to GEMME (Laine et al., 2019). GEMME outputs Mutational Landscapes for each proteoform.

  • Classification of our proteom-wide predictions using Gaussian Mixture Models into missense mutations potentially impactful, uncertain or neutral.

  • Validation of our proteom-wide predictions using different approaches.
    First, by assessing the occurence of all missense mutations amongst two different resources for genetic variation in natural populations. The Drosophila melanogaster Genetic Reference Panel (Mackay et al., 2012) gives us access to natural polymorphism fixed in 200 inbred lines while the Drosophila Evolution over Space and Time (Kapun et al., 2021; Nunez et al. , 2024) gives us access to natural polymorphism occuring in the wild (sampling period 2009 - 2021).
    Second, by confronting our predictions to the FlyBase dataset of developmentally lethal mutations and referenced hypomorphs.
    Finally, we use existing databases for protein Post Translational Modifications and Short Linear Motifs to interpret the residues predicted as sensitive or tolerant.
