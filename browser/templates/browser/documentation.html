ProteoCast Documentation

The ProteoCast Server allows you to rapidly and easily explore how missense mutations can functionaly affect proteins. ProteoCast primary source of information are the protein sequence alignements used by GEMME (Laine et al., 2019) running in the pipeline described in (Abakarova et al., 2023).

You want to know more, collaborate, or you encounter a problem with the server? Please feel free to contact us!

Input

Please provide your Multiple Sequence Alignement using CollabFold. Optionally, you can provide Uniprot ID (e.g. P07643) or upload a PDB file for mapping ProteoCast predictions onto the 3D model of the protein. Ideally, 100 or more sequences should be used for the MSA to allow relevant predictions (Abakarova et al., 2023).

Output

Please check our example result page.

Ases provides a comprehensive visualization of the results with pre-tuned parameters (nodes and edges colors and shapes) and with clear connections between the different representations of the biological objects (graph, forest of trees, multiple sequence alignments, sequences, genes and tables with ids and annotations).

GEMME mutational landscape

This interactive graph summarizes the transcript variability observed for the query gene across the selected species. Each square corresponds to a single amino acid substitution and its predicted impact score is color coded.

By hovering the mouse cursor on the representation, a contextual window will indicate the precise mutation (here P244H) and its calculated score (here -6.84).

Raw Predicted Score distributions

To systematically and automatically evaluate the agreement between our evolutionary-based mutational effect estimates and the known annotations, we classified all variants into three categories: impactful, uncertain or neutral. We achieved this by fitting a mixture of three Gaussians to GEMME predicted score distribution using the expectation-maximization algorithm. This Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) was used to tailor the classification of each protein’s individual mutational landscape. To minimize biases, we discarded low-confidence predictions.

Multiple Sequences Alignement (MSA) quality assessment

This panel will show you the MSA coverage.

Protein Segmentation profile for identifying local mutational impact biases

This plot shows the moving average GEMME score along the protein with automatically identified segments characterized by local high GEMME average. Within these segments, the functional impact of missense mutations is on average higher than in the surrounding regions.

Localisation of mutations on the 3D structure

This interactive 3D model of the protein allows you to explore the localisation of your chosen mutation in its conformational context.
